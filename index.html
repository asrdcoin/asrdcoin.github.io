On-transfer mechanics (example)

A small transfer fee routes a portion to burn and a portion to the treasury to fund buybacks, liquidity and grants. These parameters are configurable by governance within defined safety caps.

Example: feeBasis = 200 (2.00%)
- burn = 1%  (permanent removal)
- treasury = 1% (ecosystem support & buybacks)

Security & Governance

Security-first: external audits, a public bug bounty, multisig treasury and timelock governance are core requirements before major listings or integrations.

Planned Actions

  • External audit before major activity
  • Bug bounty program
  • Gnosis Safe multisig treasury
  • Timelock for critical actions (48–72 hours)

Governance Path

Founder-led multisig initially, with progressive migration to token-weighted on-chain governance. Off-chain proposal forum and snapshot signaling for early community input.

Roadmap

  1. Token finalization, website & landing page (done)
  2. Deploy token on BSC-compatible network and seed liquidity
  3. Audit, bug bounty and staking UI
  4. Exchange listings & developer grants
  5. Progressive decentralization & DAO tooling
